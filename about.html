Mountain Pose

Mountain Pose improves your posture and body awareness, strengthens your legs, and establishes good alignment

Tree Pose

Downward Dog

Downward Dog, or Adho Mukha Svanasana, stretches the hamstrings, calves, and spine while strengthening the arms and shoulders. It improves flexibility, posture, and balance, and also boosts circulation to the brain, enhancing mental clarity and reducing stress. This pose helps engage the core and promotes a sense of grounding.

Warrior I

Warrior I strengthens and stretches your legs and buttocks (glutes), the front of your hips (hip flexors), and shins.

Warrior II

The posture strengthens your core, including your abs and back muscles, as well as the hip flexors, gluteal muscles, inner thigh, and ankle of the bent leg. In your back leg, the pose stretches the hip flexors, hamstring, and calf muscles.

Chair Pose

Chair Pose, or Utkatasana, strengthens the thighs, calves, and core while improving balance and stability. It helps enhance posture and flexibility by engaging the lower body and stretching the spine. This pose also stimulates the heart and lungs, boosting circulation and energy levels. Practicing Chair Pose can aid in building endurance and promoting mental focus.
